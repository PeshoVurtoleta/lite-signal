Notes on the retained heap column:
  - Negative values mean V8 compacted reclaimable memory below the pre-bench
    baseline during the post-iteration forceGc() — i.e. no leak.
  - `lite-signal` shows +71KB retained on KAIROS because the live reactive
    graph (1 signal + 1000 computeds + 1 effect → ~70KB of pool slots) is
    pinned in the pre-allocated pool for the duration of the run. The pool
    IS the working memory — that is the design. On the other benches the
    graph is smaller (≤258 nodes) and the same pool capacity floats below
    the baseline.

Notes on Δheap (transient allocation pressure during the timed loop):
  - `lite-signal` allocates ~15KB across 20,000 iterations on every scenario
    except KAIROS (where the static graph itself accounts for the difference).
    This is the zero-GC contract: hot paths use only pre-existing pool slots.
  - The other libraries vary by orders of magnitude. preact runs ~230KB per
    20K loop. solid runs into single-digit megabytes. alien-signals — which
    in earlier measurements held to the ~15KB zero-GC band like lite-signal —
    now allocates 0.9-3.9MB depending on workload (newer versions appear to
    have changed their internal representation). This is not a leak (retained
    is near zero everywhere) but it is genuine GC pressure on the hot path.

Architectural reading:
  - lite-signal wins MUX (fan-in aggregation), BROADCAST (fan-out from one
    source), and KAIROS (one source feeding a wide layer of memos). These
    are the patterns that dominate real UI workloads.
  - alien-signals retains a 15% lead on 256-deep computed pipelines — its
    flatter internal representation pays off when the propagation path is
    long rather than wide.
  - On allocation pressure, lite-signal is now alone in the zero-Δheap band
    across the matrix. That is the differentiator: same-or-better throughput
    than every alternative across most workloads, AND no transient garbage
    generated during sustained writes.
